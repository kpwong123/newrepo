Weapons

There are three types of weapons in fencing, namely Foil, Sabre and Epee. Each of the weapons has its own appearance, rules and tactics. While one can choose to play all three weapons, professional athletes usually prefer mastering their skills in one weapon.

Foil

Foil is a weapon with a light body and a small guard. It is a thrusting weapon which is used to target opponent's torso. Hitting the head, hands and legs are considered misses such that the player does not score points with these invalid targets. The rule of 'Right of Way', which is exclusive to Foil and Sabre, allows referee to determine which player has the priority to score points. Typically, the player who attacks first and remains aggressive throughout the phase gets the priority to score points. However, if the opponent parries the blade during the attack and hits a valid target, he then gains the priority to score a point.

Epee

Epee is a thrusting weapon with a relatively heavier body and a larger guard covering the hand. Two grips, namely the pistol grip and the French grip, are commonly used among Epee fencers. Unlike Foil, valid targets in Epee cover the whole body including the head, legs and hands. Epee has no rules of 'Right of way', therefore resulting in a much slower pace during the game.

Sabre

Sabre is the only thrusting and cutting weapon in fencing in which players are required to slash or hit the upper body of the opponent in order to be awarded. Sabre has a light blade with a guard similar to a cavalry sword which extends to the tip of the handle. Because of its relative ease for players to hit a valid target, it has the most fast-paced and aggressive games in fencing. Players constantly move along the piste in order to avoid being hit and also to draw mistakes from their opponents.
